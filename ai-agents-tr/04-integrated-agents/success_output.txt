RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) is a framework that combines retrieval and generation components to enhance the performance of language models. The retrieval component fetches relevant documents or information from a large corpus, while the generation component produces coherent and contextually appropriate responses based on the retrieved content. According to the knowledge base, RAG enhances traditional generation models by incorporating retrieved context, improving accuracy and relevance in tasks like question-answering and chatbots. It is particularly useful when access to up-to-date information is critical, as it allows models to dynamically integrate external knowledge during inference. RAG models are trained on large datasets and utilize techniques such as neural networks and attention mechanisms to effectively combine retrieval and generation processes. The approach addresses the limitations of traditional models by enabling them to access external information, thereby improving their ability to handle complex and context-dependent queries.
